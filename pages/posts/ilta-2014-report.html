title: Domo Arigato, Mr. Roboto? headline: Future strategies for lawyers in an increasingly "techno-centric" world. author: Yael Citro date: 04-06-2014 coverImg : http://lp-static.s3.amazonaws.com/bionicleg.jpg {% extends "post.html" %} {% block body %} {% filter markdown %} Fast Future Research has released its report on future strategies for lawyers in an increasingly "techno-centric" world. The report highlights that in the face of increasingly powerful and global economic and technological forces, client expectations of law firms will evolve rapidly with a particular emphasis on four key themes:

1. _The Client is the Priority_ - Firms must adopt a truly client-centric approach - focusing strategy, effort and investment on securing and enhancing customer relationships.

2. _Leveraging Lawyers_ - Taking advantage of innovative collaboration tools, mobile solutions and increasingly intelligent systems to enhance the productivity, strategic insight and impact of professional staff.

3. _Re-Engineering Processes_ - Applying rigorous project management to matter execution, eliminating wasteful activity, streamlining processes and automating workflows.

4. _Innovate to Differentiate_ - Using IT to generate and support client-focused innovation.

Key findings from the report highlight that:

- _91%_ of respondants expect the transparency of the legal process to increase as a result of client demands.

- _73%_ of respondants agree or strongly agree that the capacity for rapid IT-enabled innovation will be a critical differentiator for law firms in the future.

- _77%_ of respondants agree to some extent that technology firms will increasingly enter the legal industry, using disruptive innovations to provide direct legal service delivery.

The report is the culmination of a year-long study undertaken for the International Legal Technology Association (ILTA).

The complete 140 page report can be found [here](http://www.iltanet.org/Downloads/LTFH-Report.pdf). {% endfilter %} {% endblock body %}
