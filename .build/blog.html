Domo Arigato, Mr. Roboto?

Future strategies for lawyers in an increasingly "techno-centric" world.


Fast Future Research has released its report on future strategies for lawyers in an increasingly "techno-centric" world.

The report highlights that in the face of increasingly powerful and global economic and technological forces, client expectations of law firms will evolve rapidly with a particular emphasis on four key themes:

1. The Client is the Priority - Firms must adopt a truly client-centric approach - focusing strategy, effort and investment on securing and enhancing customer relationships.

2. Leveraging Lawyers - Taking advantage of innovative collaboration tools, mobile solutions and increasingly intelligent systems to enhance the productivity, strategic insight and impact of professional staff.

3. Re-Engineering Processes - Applying rigorous project management to matter execution, eliminating wasteful activity, streamlining processes and automating workflows.

4. Innovate to Differentiate - Using IT to generate and support client-focused innovation.

Key findings from the report highlight that:

- 91% of respondants expect the transparency of the legal process to increase as a result of client demands.

- 73% of respondants agree or strongly agree that the capacity for rapid IT-enabled innovation will be a critical differentiator for law firms in the future.

- 77% of respondants agree to some extent that technology firms will increasingly enter the legal industry, using disruptive innovations to provide direct legal service delivery.

The report is the culmination of a year-long study undertaken for the International Legal Technology Association (ILTA).
