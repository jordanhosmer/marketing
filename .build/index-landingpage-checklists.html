Don't drop the ball.

The best way for lawyers to eliminate avoidable mistakes.

Nearly 75% of all malpractice suits against lawyers are because someone dropped the ball: inadequate discovery, planning or procedure errors, losing documents and other avoidable mistakes. The simple truth is that whether you are a doctor, a newspaper journalist, or a lawyer - you need to organize your thoughts. Lawyers need a strategy for making sure that no one drops the ball and the answer is ridiculously simple - it begins with a checklist.

Checklists

The greatest stress on any deal is that someone will drop the ball. Create a checklist for each client matter and remind everyone of important and critical steps.

Collaboration

Legal work is inherently collaborative. Get everyone onboard and see your deal progress in real time. You’ll always see the big picture. Never ask “Who’s doing what?” or “What’s the holdup?” again.

Centralized Discussion

Keep your team communications in one place. No more notepads, emails or post-it reminders stuck to your computer screen. LawPal keeps everyone up to date with detailed activity reports.

Cloud Sync and Storage

Import your documents from any cloud storage platform and sync final right back there.

Traffic Control

Define and delegate work, establish deadlines, review work in progress, and evaluate what’s left to do.



Document Annotation

Trustworthy and Secure
